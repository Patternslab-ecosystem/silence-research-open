# H02: Fibonacci-Timed Breath Cycles Increase Flow State Frequency

**ID:** H02
**Status:** Active Investigation
**Opened:** 2026-Q1

## Hypothesis

Breath cycle timing following Fibonacci proportions (inhale 3000ms,
hold 1854ms, exhale 4854ms) produces higher FLOW_STATE event frequency
compared to equal-duration cycles (3000ms/3000ms/3000ms).

## Rationale

The Fibonacci sequence's relationship to φ suggests that φ-proportioned
temporal patterns may better align with natural biological rhythms,
reducing the effort required to enter sustained attentional states.

## Measurement Protocol

1. Two cohorts complete 5-minute breathing sessions with different timing
2. FLOW_STATE and BREATH_CYCLE events recorded via SilenceEventV2
3. Flow state frequency compared between cohorts
4. Effect size reported as Cohen's d

## Current Status

Timing parameters implemented in PhiBreathSurface component.
Baseline observation protocol pending IRB-equivalent review.
